# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Prospective AI/ML consulting clients and companies — technical decision-makers (CTOs, heads of data, engineering leads) and the hiring/procurement people around them — evaluating whether Osama Feshier can deliver production data-science, ML, LLM, and multi-agent work. They arrive from LinkedIn, referrals, or a direct link, usually on a quick first-pass scan, asking one question: "Can this person solve our problem and ship it?" Their job-to-be-done is to size up credibility and impact fast, then find a reason and a way to make contact.

## Product Purpose

A single-page personal site that positions Osama as a senior data scientist / AI engineer available for consulting. Success is a qualified inbound contact: a visitor reads the impact (quantified results, real projects, production experience), trusts the depth (skills, experience, education), and reaches out. The site exists to convert "I found this person" into "I want to talk to this person." Everything else — the YouTube tutorials, the papers — is supporting evidence of expertise, not the primary call to action.

## Brand Personality

AI-confident and impact-driven. Three words: **confident, technical, results-oriented**. The voice speaks in outcomes ("27% greater ticket reduction," "72% outage detection," "98% accuracy"), not adjectives. It's the engineer who has shipped to production and can prove it. Tone is direct and assured without bravado — competence stated plainly. The "data-lab" aesthetic (dark surface, precise mono accents, measured motion) signals technical seriousness; it should read as a senior practitioner's workspace, never as a toy or a showcase of effects.

## Anti-references

- **Gamified / flashy portfolios** — no game-like interactions, gratuitous 3D, particle playgrounds, or look-what-I-can-animate effects. Motion serves comprehension and polish, never spectacle.
- **Crowded CV-dump pages** — not a wall of every skill, course, and certificate. Curate for impact.
- **Generic SaaS / template landing pages** — no hero-metric template clichés borrowed wholesale, no interchangeable card-grid filler.
- **Corporate-stiff LinkedIn-clone stiffness** — professional, but with a distinct point of view, not faceless.

The target is professional and confident: a serious practitioner's site that a CTO would trust with a real budget.

## Design Principles

1. **Show impact, don't claim it.** Lead with quantified, verifiable results. Numbers and shipped work carry the credibility; adjectives don't.
2. **Professional confidence over spectacle.** Every effect must earn its place by improving clarity or polish. If a visitor notices the animation before the message, it's wrong.
3. **Practice what you preach.** A craftsman's site reflects craftsmanship — precise spacing, clean hierarchy, fast load, no rough edges. The site itself is a work sample.
4. **Built for the quick scan.** Decision-makers skim. Hierarchy, scannable results, and an obvious path to contact must survive a 20-second read.
5. **Make contact effortless.** The path from "convinced" to "in touch" is short, obvious, and frictionless on every screen.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text and the muted-on-dark palette must hold ≥4.5:1 contrast (verify the `--muted` / `--faint` tokens against their backgrounds, the common dark-theme failure). All interactive elements keyboard-reachable with visible focus states; the existing skip-link stays. Honor `prefers-reduced-motion` (already wired) for the scroll reveals, animated skill bars, and floating badges. Maintain semantic landmarks and meaningful alt text.
